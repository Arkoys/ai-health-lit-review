### ⭐⭐⭐ | 3/3 | [Diverging Regulatory DNA in Adaptive Medical AI: US Agility vs EU Accountability](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fmed.2026.xxx/full)

**Lee JH, Choi B, Jeong K** | *Front Med (Lausanne)* | 2026

**Themes:** `ADAPTIVE REGULATION` · `AI GOVERNANCE` · `LIFECYCLE GOVERNANCE`

> **Why it matters:** First systematic comparison of how the US FDA and EU MDR handle continuously-learning medical devices. US allows "Predetermined Change Control Plans" — manufacturers pre-specify allowed modifications. EU MDR locks devices post-certification.

| | 🇺🇸 United States | 🇪🇺 European Union |
|---|---|---|
| **Approach** | Agile / iterative | Accountability / pre-market |
| **Mechanism** | Predetermined Change Control Plans | Lock-down post-certification |
| **Innovation pathway** | Faster iteration | Higher barriers |
| **Risk model** | Continuous monitoring | One-time assessment |

**Bottom line:** These divergent approaches will shape where adaptive medical AI innovation happens geographically. Policy implications for EU competitiveness and patient access are significant.

### ⭐⭐⭐ | 3/3 | [Learning, Potential, and Retention: Evaluating Adaptive AI-Enabled Medical Devices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04878)

**Burgon A, Sahiner B, Petrick NA** | *arXiv* | arXiv:2604.04878 | Apr 6, 2026

**Themes:** `AI EVAL/VALID` · `POST-DEPLOYMENT MONITORING` · `CONTINUOUS EVALUATION`

> **Core contribution:** Proposes a 3-axis evaluation framework to disentangle what actually changes when an adaptive AI medical device updates:

| Axis | What it measures | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **🔵 Learning** | Model improvement on current data | Is the model actually getting better? |
| **🟡 Potential** | Dataset-driven performance shifts | Is the data distribution changing? |
| **🔴 Retention** | Knowledge preservation across updates | Did the model forget previous capabilities? |

**Relevance to PhD:** Directly addresses the evaluation gap for adaptive clinical AI — a core challenge for any regulatory framework.

### ⭐⭐⭐ | 3/3 | [From Pilot Trap to Institutional Capacity: A Governance Framework for Sustainable Clinical AI](https://www.jmir.org/2026/xxx)

**Tian J, Zhao Z, Tang L** | *J Med Internet Res* | Apr 6, 2026

**Themes:** `ADAPTIVE REGULATION` · `PARTICIPATORY GOV` · `AI OVERSIGHT`

> **Core problem:** Clinical AI pilots succeed → but fail to scale institutionally. Why?

**Governance phases identified:**
1. **Pilot phase** — Experimentation, small scope, champion-driven
2. **Transition phase** — Political negotiation, resource allocation (⚠️ where most projects stall)
3. **Institutional capacity** — Embedded governance, sustained funding, integration into clinical workflows

**Implication:** Participatory governance mechanisms are critical during the transition phase — stakeholders must be involved, not just technical teams.
