## What the Paper Claims

1. **Pilot trap**: AI systems succeed in pilots but fail to transition to routine care — this is a governance failure, not a technical failure
2. **Institutional capacity argument**: Governance capacity is built *through* implementation, not pre-established before deployment
3. **6-module governance framework** derived from 18 months of fieldwork:
   - Institutional carrier formation
   - Infrastructure governance
   - Regulatory and ethical governance
   - Interdisciplinary coordination
   - Translational scaling
   - Lifecycle evaluation and oversight
4. **Functional transferability**: The framework describes observable governance conditions, not a prescriptive checklist
5. **Complement not substitute**: Positions framework as upstream complement to FDA/EU standards, which specify *what* but not *how*

## Strengths
- **Exceptionally strong empirical grounding** for a viewpoint paper
- "Pilot trap" is a precise, empirically-grounded concept — not just a metaphor
- 6-module framework is *observationally-derived*, not top-down
- Institutional capacity argument directly challenges FDA/EU AI Act assumptions
- "Functional transferability" is a genuinely new theoretical contribution
- Clear about scope: positions as *complement* to international standards

## Weaknesses
- Single case study — generalizability to other healthcare systems unproven
- China context (state-run, centralized) limits direct transferability to Western systems
- No patient outcome data
- "Functional transferability" mentioned but not fully elaborated
- Viewpoint framing means the governance framework is argued, not tested as hypothesis

## Role in the Review
- **PRIMARY evidence** for governance capacity gap (Review Finding #2)
- **PRIMARY evidence** for pilot trap as governance failure
- Can anchor the governance section's empirical grounding
- Key citation for the adaptive regulation theme

## Integration with Cornerstone Architecture
Tian provides the *diagnosis*: Pham identifies the problem (ethical + legal challenges), Tian explains WHY governance fails (pilot trap is governance, not technology). The 6-module framework then provides the *descriptive* foundation on which Hussein's prescriptive maturity model and Alsaigh's operational governance bridge build.

## One-Line Verdict
The strongest empirical contribution in the corpus — a genuinely new concept (pilot trap) backed by rigorous longitudinal fieldwork. Essential anchor for the review's governance argument.
