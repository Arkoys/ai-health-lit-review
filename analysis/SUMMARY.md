# Empirical Content Analysis — Summary of Findings

**Corpus:** 87 Priority 3 papers  
**Date:** 2026-06-02

## Key Findings

1. **Gap dominance:** Gap 2 (Governance Capacity) is the most frequently documented (18/87 = 20.7%), followed by Gap 1 (Ecological Validity) (13/87 = 14.9%), and Gap 3 (Pilot Trap) (8/87 = 9.2%).

2. **Dominant causal pathway:** gap1->gap2 (ecological validity failure causes governance capacity deficit) is the most documented interaction (5 papers), followed by gap2->gap3 (3 papers).

3. **All three gaps co-occur** in only 2 papers (2.3%), suggesting the gaps tend to be discussed in pairs or singly rather than as a unified structure.

4. **Concentration of gap documentation:** 25 papers (28.7%) document at least one gap; the remaining 62 (71.3%) are technical contributions without governance/evaluation/implementation critique.

5. **The framework is supported but with caveats:** The three gaps are documented independently and in pairs, but rarely as a unified structure. The framework is therefore empirically grounded but should not be presented as an inevitable cycle.

## Implications for Regulatory Comparison (Phase 2)

Given that:
- Gap 2 is the most documented (governance capacity is the most-cited deficit)
- gap1->gap2 is the dominant causal pathway (evaluation weakness produces governance weakness)
- The most actionable regulatory lever is therefore: addressing the *evaluation -> governance* cascade

The Phase 2 regulatory comparison should be focused on:
- Which regulatory framework is most capable of mandating ecological-validity-respecting evaluation practices?
- Which framework recognizes governance capacity as a precondition (not just an outcome) of evaluation?
- Which framework addresses the *evaluation -> governance* causal pathway, not just the individual gaps?
