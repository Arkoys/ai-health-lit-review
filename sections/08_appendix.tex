\subsection{Annotated Bibliography: Top 10 Selected Papers}
\label{sec:annotated_bib}

% The ten papers below are the most heavily cited cornerstone
% works; the complete 60-paper V3.1 corpus roster with binary
% gap coding and stance labels is provided in Section~\ref{sec:corpus_roster}.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 1 — Pham (2025): Ethical and legal considerations in healthcare AI}}
\textit{Relevance:} Covers all 5 themes. Serves as the comprehensive intro
paper for the review — bridges evaluation, governance, ethics, and implementation.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 2 — Tian et al. (2026): From Pilot Trap to Institutional Capacity}}
\textit{Relevance:} Core paper for Sub-Question II (governance structures) and
Sub-Question III (implementation barriers). Provides the empirical foundation
for the "pilot trap" concept and the 6-module governance framework.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 3 — OpenMRF (2026): Open-source MRF framework}}
\textit{Relevance:} Sub-Question I (evaluation methods) — demonstrates
reproducibility and open-science evaluation approaches in imaging AI.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 4 — Bailes et al. (2026): TRAC-Pain Protocol}}
\textit{Relevance:} Sub-Question I (evaluation methods) — methodological
template for prospective, longitudinal, ecologically grounded evaluation.
Demonstrates how evaluation methodology can be designed to capture
real-world clinical complexity.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 5 — Warraich, Tazbaz \& Califf (2024): FDA Perspective}}
\textit{Relevance:} Sub-Question II (regulatory governance) — authoritative
regulatory overview. Essential reading for the regulatory landscape
(FDA and international coordination) section.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 6 — Bouderhem (2025): Shaping the future of AI in healthcare through ethics and governance}}
\textit{Relevance:} Sub-Question II (participatory governance) — ethics-first
approach emphasizing community engagement and accountability mechanisms.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 7 — Abdul (2025): AI-Driven Ethical Governance}}
\textit{Relevance:} Sub-Question II (adaptive governance) — balance between
innovation and accountability across data-intensive sectors including healthcare.
Useful for comparative sectoral perspective.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 8 — Kéri (2026): Simulated consciousness in psychiatry AI}}
\textit{Relevance:} Sub-Question III (implementation barriers, safety risks) and
Sub-Question II (governance) — unique contribution on psychiatric AI failure
modes (simulated empathy, illusion of attachment) not addressed elsewhere.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{\underline{Paper 9 — Abulibdeh, Celi \& Sejdić (2025): Illusion of safety — FDA report}}
\textit{Relevance:} Sub-Question I (evaluation — post-market monitoring gap)
and Sub-Question II (regulatory governance) — direct critique of FDA approval
process, calls for adaptive community-engaged regulation. Essential counterpoint
to Warraich et al.'s FDA perspective.
